<h1>Search</h1>

<p>Use this form to search the corpus. The search engine only supports single-word queries against a limited number of indicies. Examples include: <a href="./search.cgi?q=art&i=titles">art in title words</a>, <a href="./search.cgi?q=science">science</a>, <a href="./search.cgi?q=knowledge">knowledge</a>, and <a href="./search.cgi?q=love">love</a></p>

<p>But really consider perusing/downloading the various indexes (<a href="./etc/index-authors.idx">authors</a>, <a href="./etc/index-dates.idx">dates</a>, <a href="./etc/index-facets.idx">facets</a>, <a href="./etc/index-languages.idx">languages</a>, <a href="./etc/index-publishers.idx">publisher words</a>, <a href="./etc/index-subjects.idx">subjects</a>, and <a href="./etc/index-titles.idx">title words</a>) into your favorite spreadsheet, database, or text editing application. Once imported into such an application, you will be able to search, sort, and browse the index to get a <em>much better</em> idea of what the collection includes and how it is described. Believe me, the process will be eye-opening!</p>
